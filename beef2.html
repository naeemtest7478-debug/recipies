Beef Recipe

Recipe Information

  • Yield: 4 servings
  • More info: View Recipe Source

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (3½–4 lbs)
  • 1 Tbsp Diamond Crystal or 1¾ tsp Morton kosher salt, plus more
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 5 Tbsp gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
  • ¼ cup plus 2 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 heads of garlic
  • 1½" piece fresh ginger
  • 1½ lbs baby Yukon Gold potatoes (about 1½" in diameter)
  • 5 scallions
  • 2 limes
  • 2 tsp honey

Preparation

  1. Place a rack in the middle of the oven; preheat to 300°F. Pat chicken dry with paper towels. Season inside and out with salt and pepper.
  2. Whisk gochujang and olive oil in a bowl. Grate 3 garlic cloves and ginger into the mixture; whisk to combine.
  3. Cut remaining garlic heads in half. Stuff 2 halves into the chicken cavity. Tie legs together.
  4. Brush half the gochujang oil over the chicken.
  5. Toss potatoes and remaining garlic halves in the rest of the gochujang oil. Season lightly.
  6. Arrange potatoes in a skillet, place garlic halves in center, then set chicken over them.
  7. Roast until chicken is deep golden and potatoes are very tender (about 2½–3 hours). Let chicken rest 10–15 minutes.
  8. Smash potatoes gently to soak in juices.
  9. Finish potatoes with scallions, lime juice, and honey. Adjust salt to taste.
  10. Carve chicken, arrange over potatoes, and serve with lime wedges. Squeeze out roasted garlic as desired.
